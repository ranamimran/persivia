Assessment Tab

The Assessments tab displays questions that relate to:

 

  • An individual patient, the patient whose I.D. is displayed in the current Workflow Window.
  • A specific chronic condition for which the patient is in treatment (that is, a specific diagnosis)
  • How well the patient’s care plan corresponds to the patient’s diagnosis
  • How well the patient is complying with his or her care plan
  • How well the course of care appears to be meeting applicable Clinical Quality Measures.

 

 

Some answers to these questions can be entered immediately after a Care Coordination session with the patient. The CM system helps to facilitate care by making the Assessment questions and previous assessments available during care-coordination conferences. Beyond this, the Assessment provides a clear frame of reference for updating a care plan and for helping to carry it out.

 

Besides letting you view the current assessment, the Assessments tab allows you to view prior assessments, compare assessments, and create new assessments.  

 

To view a previous assessment, click the corresponding button at the upper left of the tab, as shown in the following detailed view:

 

 

When you do this, the single previous assessment appears. This is usually similar in form to the current assessment and the comparison allows you to observe changes in the patient’s status with respect to the diagnosis.

 

To compare assessments over a wider span of patient history, click the Compare Assessment button. When you do so, a drop-down menu displays a list of available prior assessments. The following illustration shows the menu, the user’s choice to view the assessment of 2/26/2015, and the resulting prior assessment. (This figure combines two actions; normally, the assessment showing the selected date would not appear until after you had closed the menu).

 

 

The selected prior assessment appears on the left-hand side; the current or newest, loads on the right. Both assessments are dated to facilitate comparison.  Comparing assessments can make it easier to choose your method of making a new assessment.

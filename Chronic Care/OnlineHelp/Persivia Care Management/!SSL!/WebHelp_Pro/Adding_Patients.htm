Adding Patients

To add patients, you would select Manage Patients from the Manage Patient Workflow.

 

 

The Manage Patient button is located in the upper left of the large Patient Workflow display.

 

 

When you click Manage Patient, the (MPW Window appears, displaying the New Patient button. Click the New Patient button to add new patient information.

 

 

The Patient Tab opens from the Manage Patient Workflow Window, presenting fields for basic patient ID and contact information. At the same time, you see other tabs that subsequently let you add a wide range of details from the patient record.

 

 

Enter information into each field. The following table summarizes the information to be added in the Patient Tab.

 

Tab Name Type of Information Examples of Fields
Patient Identifying information, “demographic” information, contact information for patient, support person, and legal guardian

 MRN, First Name, Last Name, Address, Phone ( home, work, mobile), DOB, Birth Place,  Marital Status, Birth Place, Language, Religion.

 

 

 

Adding a new patient also requires entering contact information and other data about the patient’s guardian, if applicable, and support person.

 

When you add a new patient, you enter information that identifies not only the patient but also his or her Insurance, Diagnosis, Medications, Vital Signs, Labs, Vaccines, Procedures, Visits, Allergies, Social History, and Family History.  Each of these information categories has a dedicated tab in the New Patient Window,

 

 

The New Patient window displays a button labeled Add at the lower right.

 

 

All the tabs that open from the Add Patient display (Insurance, Diagnosis, Medications, etc.) allow you to enter information about additional instances of the attribute in question. This case represents a patient attribute, his or her support person. Because there can be more than one instance (for example, a patient can have more than one support person), CM provides for adding others via the Add button.

When you have entered a patient’s data in these screens, the patient information is stored and run against Persivia’s Patient Evaluation Service (PES).

 

Most actions of care coordination entail focusing on an individual patient and his or her clinical history. For this reason, the Manage Patient Workflow is organized so that you can select an individual patient and then link to all care management activities for that patient. On selecting an individual patient in the Workflow, you focus that patient’s information in the Workflow Window. The next section describes its individual tabs.

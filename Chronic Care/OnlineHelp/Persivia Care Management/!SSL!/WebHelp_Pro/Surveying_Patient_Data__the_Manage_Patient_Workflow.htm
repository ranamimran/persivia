Surveying Patient Data: the Manage Patient Workflow

The main Care Management screen helps users by making essential data accessible immediately. Because managing patients requires you to survey a wide field of patient data at the outset of many tasks, CCM begins its patient-management display cycle in with a list, as shown below:

 

 

Because you also need rapid access to detail, each entry in the list is linked to a display of local detail, specific to that entry. The experience is that of a linked list; the mechanism is that of a hyperlink. The CM system organizes each set of detail “behind” an expanding list entry that operates via a hyperlink. When you click on a blue patient CMID number or its adjacent expansion arrow (as shown in the Figure below) you display the corresponding patient in a detail window (Figure 8). This window allows you to view this patient’s specific details and to perform many related actions.

 

 

When you click on the expansion arrow next to a specific patient, the Manage Patient Workflow Window appears, showing information about the specified patient.

 

 

On opening this workflow window you can access both information that you (or another user) added, and information that CCM added through its logic. The combination of information allows for precise access to patient details. Besides providing ready access to MRNs, DOBs, and other identifying facts, the main screen provides a tabular format with direct access to these values. They appear as column heads and are outlined below:

 

 

The columns in the Manage Patient Workflow give you direct access to basic facts about the patient. Once enrolled, this patient would be shown in the category (bucket) called Pending Patient Consent. When patient consent is secured, your next task as a Care Manager may be to create (draft) a care plan. Care plans that are created by users who have Care Manager status remain in draft form until approved by a physician. However, to draft a care plan or move a patient to the next step in an existing plan, you usually need to review detailed information for the specified patient.

 

The Manage Patient Workflow helps you to create draft care plans by linking you with relevant aspects of patient history, and even with elements of care plans that have been successfully used in the past.

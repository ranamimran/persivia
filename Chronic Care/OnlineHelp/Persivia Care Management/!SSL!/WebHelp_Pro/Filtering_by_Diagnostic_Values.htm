Filtering by Diagnostic Values

It is very useful to be able to select groups of patients based on the diagnostic values that appear in their records. Often open alerts and other care-management imperatives occur across diagnostic categories. For example, it may often be useful to escalate action on all patients with DM whose latest HbA1c values were above a specified level. To enable managing patients by diagnosis or by specific diagnostic value, the Care Management screen provides a pane of diagnostic filters. This group of diagnostic filters appears in the Patient Workflow for both the Care Manager and for the Care Physician. It appears on the left below the Care Plan filter group. Its filter options are illustrated below:

 

 

The Diagnoses filter includes many of the most common chronic-disease diagnoses:

 

  • Asthma
  • Afib/Aflutter
  • CKD
  • COPD
  • Depression
  • DM
  • Not Specified

 

The Filter pane also allows you to filter by specific diagnostic threshold. For example, The Lab filter allows you to select patients based on lab readings for any of the following clinical indicators:

  • FEV1
  • Oxygen Saturation
  • HbA1C
  • LDL-C
  • Albumin Creatinine Ratio
  • eGFR
  • HDL
  • Potassium

 

If you use one of these filters together with a Diagnosis filter, you can select a group of patients with great precision. For example, you may need to manage the care of all diabetic patients in your pool of eligible individuals who have HbA1c values over a specific threshold. Instead of searching through patient records, you can combine the Diagnoses filter and the HbA1c filters to identify all patients who need urgent diabetic interventions.
